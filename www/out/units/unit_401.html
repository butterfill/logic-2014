Soundness and Completeness: Restatement of the Theorems

Soundness:

if A ⊢ B then A ⊨ B

For a system of proof to be sound is for it not to permit proofs of arguments unless they are valid.

Completeness:

if A ⊨TT B then A ⊢ B

For a system of proof to be completeTT is for it to contain proofs of all arguments which are logically valid in virtue of the meanings of the truth-functional connectives.
